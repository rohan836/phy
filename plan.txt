## What is $E=mc^2$?

$E$ = Energy, $m$ = Mass, $c$ = Speed of light ($\approx 3 \times 10^8$ m/s)

In simple terms: mass is a concentrated form of energy. A tiny amount of matter can be converted into a huge amount of energy because $c^2$ is an enormous number.

$$E = mc^2$$

Example: The mass of a single paperclip, if fully converted, could release energy equivalent to a nuclear bomb — enough to power a small city for years.

This equation explains why:
- The Sun shines (nuclear fusion converts mass to energy)
- Nuclear power plants work (fission converts mass to energy)
- Atomic bombs are so devastating
